bind it to the nearest threads and keep it stretched, especially at the
mouth. Then the spinnerets take a rest and the turn of the ovaries comes. A
continuous shower of eggs falls into the bag, which is filled to the
top. The capacity of the receptacle has been so nicely calculated that
there is room for all the eggs, without leaving any space unoccupied. When the Spider has finished and retires, I catch a momentary glimpse of
the heap of orange-coloured eggs; but the work of the spinnerets is at
once resumed. The next business is to close the bag.
